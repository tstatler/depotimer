Depo Timer

A simple on-the-record timer for videographers covering Zoom depositions.

What it's for

When you're recording a deposition on Zoom, you need a clean log of when the recording was rolling — start, every break, and the final stop. Depo Timer mirrors your Zoom record button so you end the day with an accurate on-the-record total and a session-by-session breakdown you can hand off with the video.

During the deposition

  1. When you hit Record in Zoom at the start of the depo, tap the green Start / In button. The timer starts counting and a pulsing green dot shows you're on the record.
  2. When the noticing attorney calls a break (lunch, witness conference, bathroom), pause Zoom's recording and tap the red Stop / Out button at the same moment. The session length appears as a blue duration capsule.
  3. When you go back on the record, resume the Zoom recording and tap Start / In again. Repeat for every break.
  4. When the deposition concludes, stop the Zoom recording and tap Stop / Out one last time.

Fixing a missed tap

If you forgot to tap exactly when you started or stopped the recording, click any time in the log to edit it. Type the correct time as HH:MM:SS (24-hour) and press Enter to save, or Esc to cancel. Only the time changes — the date stays the same.

Wrapping up the job

After the depo is over, you can hand off your record two ways:

  • Copy — puts a readable text log on the clipboard, ready to paste into a job-sheet, email, or invoice note.
  • CSV — downloads a spreadsheet file named time-log-YYYY-MM-DD.csv, useful for billing systems or archiving alongside the video files.

Both exports list entries oldest-first, with each session's duration shown on the Out line that closes it, and a total on-the-record time at the bottom.

Starting a new day

The trash icon clears every entry so you can start fresh for the next deposition. There's no undo — export first if you still need the log.

Your data stays on your device. The web version saves to your browser's local storage; the Mac menu bar version saves to your user defaults. Nothing is uploaded.
